# Credit Card Customer Analysis (Power BI | SQL | Excel)

A data-driven case study focused on analyzing customer behavior, card usage patterns, revenue trends, and risk indicators in the credit card domain. This project demonstrates how analytics can support product, business, and risk-related decision-making.

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes credit card customer and transaction data to uncover insights related to customer acquisition, engagement, delinquency, revolving balances, and card performance. The goal was to identify business opportunities, highlight risk areas, and support data-driven decisions through an interactive Power BI dashboard.

---

## Business Objective

- Analyze customer behavior, transaction patterns, and revenue trends
- Track key KPIs such as activation rate, delinquency rate, and transaction volume
- Identify high-risk and high-value customer segments
- Support product, marketing, and risk strategy with actionable insights

---

## Tools & Technologies

- **SQL** – Data cleaning, joins, aggregations, and analysis
- **Power BI** – Dashboard development and KPI visualization
- **Excel** – Data validation and supporting analysis

---

## Key Analysis Performed

- Analyzed customer and transaction datasets to identify spending behavior and revenue trends
- Tracked activation, delinquency, and revolving balance metrics across different customer segments
- Evaluated card type performance by revenue, transaction volume, and usage patterns
- Compared customer behavior across gender, employment type, and geography
- Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to monitor business and risk KPIs

---

## Dashboard Highlights

The dashboard includes:

- KPI overview: revenue, activation rate, delinquency rate, transaction volume
- Customer segmentation by gender, employment type, and state
- Card performance analysis by transaction count and revenue contribution
- Risk indicators such as revolving balance and delinquency by segment
- Weekly and year-to-date performance tracking

---

## Key Business Insights

### Weekly Performance (Week 53 Ending 31st Dec)
- Revenue increased by **28.8%**
- Total transaction amount increased by **36.4%**
- Transaction count increased by **3.39%**
- Customer base increased by **12.8%**

### Year-to-Date Overview
- **Total Revenue:** $57M
- **Interest Earned:** $8M
- **Transaction Volume:** $46M

### Customer & Card Insights
- Male customers contributed **$31M** revenue compared to **$26M** from female customers
- Blue and Silver cards accounted for **93%** of total transactions
- Texas, New York, and California contributed **68%** of total revenue combined

### Risk & Engagement Insights
- Overall activation rate stood at **57.5%**
- Overall delinquency rate stood at **6.06%**
- Self-employed customers showed significantly higher delinquency at **25.53%**
- Total revolving balance was **75.66%**, indicating strong potential for both revenue and repayment risk analysis

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### Customer Segmentation
- Upsell salaried customers with good repayment behavior to premium card categories
- Offer secured or lower-risk card options to high-risk customer segments

### Product & Offer Strategy
- Launch activation campaigns for new customers within the first 15 days
- Create spend-based upgrade offers for highly active Blue and Silver card users
- Design EMI or repayment support offers for customers with high revolving balances

### Risk Management
- Introduce early warning indicators for high-risk customer segments
- Apply lower initial exposure for segments with high delinquency patterns
- Strengthen financial awareness campaigns through digital channels

### Geographic & Product Focus
- Increase targeted campaigns in high-performing states such as Texas, New York, and California
- Review underperforming card categories and redesign offers based on usage behavior

## Business Relevance

This project demonstrates how data can be used to answer important business questions such as:

- Which customer segments are most valuable?
- Which segments carry the highest repayment risk?
- Which card categories drive the most usage and revenue?
- Where should product and marketing teams focus to improve activation and retention?

The analysis helps bridge the gap between raw data and business action, making it relevant for **Business Analyst, Product Analyst, and BI Analyst** roles.
